Soleau
  • @SoleauLetter
  • What is Soleau?

    • Soleau is an unofficial copyright registry that leverages the Internet to prove when you actually made a creative work. Soleau sends a work that you create, with a randomly generated 10-digit id, to your email and your Twitter account. It's like sending something to yourself in the mail to show that something happened on a particular date and at a particular time.

  • Can I really get copyright protection from an email?

    • Well, no. But you do actually get copyright protection in your original work as soon as it's "fixed." That means that it is recorded somewhere--e.g., on paper, in a text editor, or on film.

  • But don't I actually have to register copyright with a government office to get "official" protection?

    • No you do not! In most countries all you need to do is actually create the work, at which point you get full copyright protection on that work.

  • So what's the point of this then?

    • This is an anologue to the "Poor Man's Copyright," which is a way of proving when something comes into existence by sending it to yourself in the mail.
