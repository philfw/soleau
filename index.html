The Modern Day

Poor Man's Copyright

The Soleau envelope is named for the French inventor Eugene Soleau. It is a fairly common method of proving when you created an original work or invention: simply seal your original plan or work into envelope, then send the envelope to yourself by post. You can then use the postmark date to prove that the work existed at that time (if you don't open the envelope!).

Soleau is a modern analogue to the Soleau envelope. Copy and paste an original work into the box below, and a plain text version of the work will be emailed directly to you with a unique ID number. At the same time, we will compose a Tweet for you with that same ID and the hashtag #Soleau. Post that Tweet to show everyone that the email we sent you is legit! ⬇
